Masu

Description

Nepali chicken curry, or *kukhura ko masu*, is a flavorful and aromatic dish that's central to Nepali cuisine. Unlike Indian curries, which can be creamier or have a heavier use of spices, Nepali chicken curry is known for its lighter, earthy taste, combining fresh ginger, garlic, turmeric, cumin, and coriander, with a modest use of tomatoes and green chilies. The chicken is often marinated in spices before cooking, allowing it to absorb a deep flavor, then slow-cooked until tender, resulting in a thick, aromatic sauce that clings beautifully to each piece of chicken.

The dish is traditionally served with steamed rice (*bhat*), pickles (*achar*), and often lentil soup (*dal*), making it a comforting part of the Nepali meal experience. Nepali chicken curry is both spicy and subtly complex, with the use of *timur* (Sichuan pepper) adding a unique, tingling warmth that sets it apart. Each household might add its own touch, sometimes with a dash of mustard oil or fenugreek seeds, lending a distinctive twist to this cherished, homestyle dish.


Ingredients


  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil (or any cooking oil)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp Sichuan pepper (timur), optional
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish

Steps

```html
  1. Heat the mustard oil in a large pot over medium heat. Allow it to get hot until it reaches a smoking point, then reduce the heat slightly.
  2. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add the minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell fades.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and blend into the mixture.
  5. Stir in the turmeric, cumin, coriander powder, and salt. Cook the spices for a minute to bring out their aroma.
  6. Add the chicken pieces, stirring well to coat them in the spice mixture. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the chicken starts to brown slightly.
  7. Pour in about 1 cup of water to create a curry sauce. Cover the pot and let the chicken simmer on low heat for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  8. Sprinkle garam masala and Sichuan pepper (if using) into the curry and stir well. Let it simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  9. Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.
