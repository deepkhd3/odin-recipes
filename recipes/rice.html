Bhat

Description

Nepali-style rice, or bhat, is a staple side dish that accompanies almost every Nepali meal. The rice is typically cooked plain and served with dishes like daal (lentil soup), curries, and pickles. The simplicity of bhat allows it to balance the rich flavors of Nepali curries and gravies, providing a mild, fluffy base that soaks up spices and sauces beautifully. While plain white rice is most common, sometimes basmati or long-grain rice is used for a more fragrant result.

In traditional Nepali cooking, rice is often rinsed thoroughly to remove excess starch, resulting in soft, separate grains. Itâ€™s boiled or steamed with just water and a bit of salt, then fluffed up before serving. Some households may add a splash of ghee for added richness. Simple yet essential, Nepali rice is versatile and comforting, a perfect canvas for the vibrant flavors of a Nepali meal.


Ingredients


  • 1 cup white rice (basmati or any preferred variety)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp salt (optional)
  • 1 tsp ghee or oil (optional, for extra richness)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly in cold water 2-3 times to remove excess starch, then drain.
  2. In a medium pot, add the rinsed rice, water, and salt (if using). Stir gently.
  3. Place the pot over medium-high heat and bring the water to a boil.
  4. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the water is absorbed and the rice is tender.
  5. If using, add a teaspoon of ghee or oil, and gently fluff the rice with a fork to mix it in.
  6. Turn off the heat, keep the rice covered for 5 more minutes to allow it to steam, then fluff again before serving.
